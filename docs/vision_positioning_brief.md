## Vision & Positioning Brief

This document frames SuperStop’s target audience, promise, and go-to-market positioning so product, design, and growth teams can align on what makes the app distinct for kids with ADHD and their caregivers.

### Primary Personas

**Maya (11) – Middle Schooler with ADHD**
- Goals: Finish homework before dinner, build confidence in self-control, earn tangible rewards she can show her parents.
- Frustrations: Easily overwhelmed by multi-step tasks, impatient waiting for turn in class, frequent negative feedback.
- Triggers/Needs: Bright visuals and quick wins keep her engaged; needs very short sessions (≤5 min) and clear cues for success.
- App Opportunities: Gamified impulse-control drills, streak tracking to celebrate progress, breathing exercises to reset after setbacks.

**Noam (9) – Elementary Student Who Loves Playful Tech**
- Goals: Channel energy between school and after-school activities, gain praise from teachers, show mastery to siblings.
- Frustrations: Bored by static content, struggles with reading-heavy instructions, loses focus without audio prompts.
- Triggers/Needs: Responsive feedback (sounds, haptics), voiceover-friendly flows, minimal text with iconography.
- App Opportunities: High-energy mini-games with escalating difficulty, audio-guided breathing, customizable avatars/coins to reflect progress.

**Dana (38) – Parent & Coach Facilitator**
- Goals: Support child’s regulation routines, monitor progress without micro-managing, share wins with therapists/teachers.
- Frustrations: Limited time to research tools, mistrust of apps without evidence, wants privacy assurances.
- Triggers/Needs: Simple dashboards, digestible weekly summaries, optional caregiver notifications, clear data policies.
- App Opportunities: Parent view of streaks and goal completion, downloadable reports, onboarding that positions app as trusted partner.

### Problem & Value Proposition

- **Core Problem**: Kids with ADHD and their caregivers struggle to find repeatable, fun routines that improve impulse control and emotional regulation at home. Existing tools lean on static checklists, lack immediate feedback, and rarely motivate kids to return daily without heavy parental oversight.
- **SuperStop Promise**: A playful training companion that turns impulse-control practice into quick, rewarding missions—helping kids stay engaged while giving caregivers confidence in measurable progress.
- **One-Liner**: “SuperStop keeps kids with ADHD on track through bite-sized games, calming tools, and streaks that celebrate every win.”
- **Expanded Pitch**: “SuperStop combines therapist-informed impulse-control games, guided breathing, and progress dashboards into a single bilingual app. Kids hop in for five-minute missions, earn coins and achievements, and see their streaks grow. Parents and coaches get simple summaries that prove the routines are working—no nagging required.”

### Success Metrics & Analytics Needs

- **North-Star Metric**: Weekly Active Engaged Kids (WAEK) — number of unique child accounts completing ≥3 regulated activities (game, timer, or breathing) per week.
- **Core Supporting Metrics**
  - Daily streak retention: % of active kids maintaining a streak ≥3 days.
  - Session completion rate: ratio of sessions started vs. finished for each exercise type.
  - Caregiver insight adoption: % of caregivers viewing the dashboard weekly.
  - Mood check-in adherence: % of prompted check-ins completed after sessions.
- **Health Metrics**
  - Crash-free sessions per platform.
  - Average session length (target 3–7 minutes) to preserve short-form engagement.
  - Time-to-first-activity post-onboarding (<2 minutes target).
- **Instrumentation Checklist**
  - Implement analytics events for: onboarding step completion, activity start/finish, streak increments, achievements earned, caregiver dashboard views, localization preference changes.
  - Capture context properties: activity type, duration brackets, mood score, reward type, language, child vs. caregiver view.
  - Configure cohort tagging for new vs. returning users, and ADHD diagnosis confirmation (if optionally provided).
  - Set up automated dashboards/alerts for streak drop-offs and crash spikes.

### Brand Tone & Messaging Cues

- **Voice Pillars**
  - Encouraging coach: Celebrate progress, use affirmative language (“You did it!”, “Ready for the next mission?”).
  - Playfully focused: Blend excitement with clarity; pair energetic verbs with simple instructions.
  - Trustworthy partner: Communicate evidence-backed intent, highlight caregiver controls and privacy guardrails.
- **Bilingual Consistency**
  - Mirror tone in Hebrew and English; avoid idioms that do not translate cleanly.
  - Keep sentences short (≤12 words) to support emerging readers and screen readers.
- **Key Phrases**
  - “Mission complete”, “Streak saved”, “Take a calming breath”, “Coach tips”.
  - Avoid deficit framing (e.g., “struggle”, “problem child”); focus on strengths and progress.
- **Visual & Audio Direction**
  - Use bright gradients and friendly characters for kid-facing screens; adopt muted accent palette for caregiver dashboards.
  - Pair animations with soft chimes/positive auditory cues; ensure optional mute for sensory-sensitive users.
  - Maintain high-contrast typography and include icon + text for critical actions.
- **Copy Patterns**
  - Activity prompts: “Inhale... hold... exhale” / “Tap only when the circle shines green.”
  - Reward feedback: “You earned 5 coins for focused breathing!”
  - Caregiver summary: “Maya completed 3 focus missions this week. Her longest streak is 5 days.”

### Validation & Feedback Loop

1. **Internal Alignment Sprint (Week 1)**
   - Share personas, value prop, and voice guidelines with product, design, and clinical advisors.
   - Collect async comments; host a 45-minute workshop to resolve messaging conflicts.
2. **Rapid Caregiver Interviews (Weeks 2–3)**
   - Recruit 5–7 parents/caregivers via existing community channels.
   - Test positioning one-pager and dashboard mock snippets; capture clarity, trust, and differentiation scores (1–5 scale).
3. **Kid Playtest Sessions (Weeks 3–4)**
   - Run 3 moderated 20-minute sessions with target-age kids using prototype copy and flows.
   - Observe comprehension of prompts, engagement with rewards messaging, and emotional response.
4. **Quant Pulse Survey (Week 4)**
   - In-app micro-survey after onboarding: “Did this feel made for you?” (Yes/No + optional why).
   - Target ≥75% positive; if below, analyze qualitative feedback and iterate copy.
5. **Iteration Cadence**
   - Document findings in shared brief, update messaging guidelines quarterly.
   - Re-run targeted interviews before major content releases or localization expansions.
